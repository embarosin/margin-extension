Margin — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2026-06-29

Margin is a browser extension that lets you leave comments on web pages. This policy explains exactly what data Margin handles and where it goes. Margin has no analytics, no tracking, no ads, and no third-party data sharing.

What Margin stores, and where

  • Your comments and identity (display name, avatar color, comment text, and the page anchors they attach to) are stored locally in your browser via the storage API. They never leave your device unless you enable Team Sync.
  • Your settings (including any API key you enter) are stored locally in your browser. The API key is never transmitted to us or anyone except the AI provider you choose (see below).

We, the developers, operate no servers and receive none of your data.

Optional features that send data off your device

These are off by default. Data leaves your browser only if you turn them on:

  1. AI (optional). You choose the provider — Anthropic, OpenAI, Google Gemini, or any OpenAI-compatible endpoint (e.g. Azure OpenAI, a local model). If you add a key and use an AI feature, the extension sends — directly from your browser to the provider you selected — the relevant page text, the comment thread, and your question, in order to generate a response. This happens only when you click an AI action. Each provider's own privacy policy governs that data (e.g. Anthropic, OpenAI, Google). Your API key is sent only to that provider, only to authenticate these requests.
  2. Team Sync (optional). If you enter a Sync Server URL, your comments for a page are sent to that server, which you or your team operate. Margin does not run this server and does not control it; data handling there is governed by whoever hosts it. If you leave this blank, no comment data is ever transmitted.

What Margin does NOT do

  • No analytics, telemetry, or usage tracking.
  • No advertising or ad networks.
  • No selling, renting, or sharing of personal data.
  • No collection of browsing history. Margin reads page content only on pages where you actively use it, and only to anchor your comments or (if enabled) to answer an AI question you asked.

Permissions and why they're needed

  • storage — to save your comments and settings locally.
  • contextMenus — to add the right-click "Comment on this page" entry.
  • identity (optional) — only used if you sign in to your team's sync server via Google.
  • Host access (<all_urls>) — so you can comment on any page, including internal tools and localhost. Margin's UI is injected on pages but stays inert until you turn it on. It does not read or send page content in the background.

Data deletion

Open Settings → Data → "Delete all Margin comments in this browser", or remove the extension, to erase all locally stored data. For Team Sync, ask your server operator to delete server-side data.
